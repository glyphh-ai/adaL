# Learning toward AGI — the curriculum thesis

The argument: a substrate with a fixed universal schema + a recursive
primitive lattice + zero hallucination + auditable traversal becomes
qualitatively different from any LLM when you scale the curriculum.

LLMs are interpolators. They predict the next token from statistical
patterns over text. They cannot tell you *why* they said something,
they cannot enumerate what they know, and they cannot refuse without
guessing. **Ada can.**

Push the curriculum past **1 billion grounded tokens** and Ada doesn't
become "another LLM with more memory." It becomes a different category
of system — one where every concept is traceable to the cognitive
primitives it composes from, where every answer cites the specific
facts that produced it, and where "I don't know" is a structural
property of the substrate's primitive chain reach, not a politeness
heuristic.

## The five things this curriculum is designed to make Ada do

These are operations *no LLM can perform*, on *any* corpus, no matter
how much was in training:

1. **Trace any concept to its grounding primes.** "What is
   `food.apple` composed of, all the way down?" → returns the
   recursive BFS through the lattice until everything is a layer-0
   prime. Auditable, deterministic, byte-identical across runs.

2. **Enumerate what's known about a primitive.** "Show me every
   concept that depends on `prime.see`." → returns the set of all
   higher-layer concepts (`color.blue`, `action.see`, `body.eye`,
   ...) by relational query over the substrate.

3. **Refuse with provenance.** "What does `chemistry.benzene` taste
   like?" → if no `taste` role is filled in the substrate's
   benzene-chain, refuse explicitly, citing which primes the chain
   passes through and where the gap is. *Not* a guess; a structural
   "no path."

4. **Cross-domain compositional bind.** "Find concepts that have
   `perceptual.color = green` AND `prime.live` in their composition
   chain." → returns plants, certain animals, certain microbes — by
   role-level intersection over a substrate that grew from
   independently-taught domains.

5. **Generate from primitive composition.** Given a target glyph in
   universal-schema form, recursively unfold its composition to
   produce a grounded description that traces back to NSM primes.
   This is the inverse of (1), and is what "explainable cognition"
   actually looks like.

## The layer plan

| Layer | What | Count target | Token target | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | NSM semantic primes | 63 | ~1K | ✓ built |
| 1 | perceptual / numerical / spatial / logical / temporal | 59 + 120 comps | ~10K | ✓ built |
| 2 | physical world (animals, plants, materials, foods, body parts, tools) | ~500 | ~50K | **starting here** |
| 3 | actions and processes (motion, manipulation, mental, social, biological) | ~300 | ~30K | starting here |
| 4 | mental and social concepts (emotion, belief, promise, ownership) | ~500 | ~50K | planned |
| 5 | physics + math + biology + chemistry primitives | ~2,000 | ~200K | planned |
| 6 | domain bodies (each natural science, engineering, history, language) | ~50,000 | ~10M | planned |
| 7 | applied / contextual knowledge (laws, recipes, procedures) | ~500,000 | ~100M | planned |
| 8 | encyclopedic facts (Wikidata-scale) | ~50,000,000 | **~1B+** | planned |

The early layers are **hand-written exemplars** that anchor each
domain. Once the primitive structure is firm, the higher layers are
generated by LLM-assisted curriculum extraction from structured
sources (WikiData, ConceptNet, WordNet, OpenAlex, etc.) mapped into
the universal-schema lattice via `tell_raw`.

## Honest cost projection

| Stage | Curation effort | LLM cost (Haiku) | Wall time | Disk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Layers 0–3 (this thread) | hand-written | $0 (no LLM at ingest) | hours | < 1 MB |
| Layer 4–5 (planned) | hand-written w/ LLM assistance | < $100 | days | < 100 MB |
| Layer 6 (planned) | LLM-assisted from structured sources | ~$5K | weeks | 1-10 GB |
| Layer 7 (planned) | LLM-assisted with verification loop | ~$50K | months | 50-100 GB |
| Layer 8 (planned) | structured-source ingestion | ~$500K-$1M | year | 500 GB - 1 TB |
| **Total to ~1B tokens** | | **~$0.5-1M** | **~12 months** | **~1 TB** |

For comparison: a single GPT-4-class pretraining run is ~$100M. Ada's
path to 1B grounded tokens with full audit and zero hallucination is
two orders of magnitude cheaper. The trade is curation effort —
which is *exactly* the cost of producing knowledge that is
*verifiable* rather than *plausible*.

## What we're building in this thread, right now

1. **Layer 2**: ~50 hand-written entries spanning animals, plants,
   materials, foods, body parts, tools. Each cites the layer-0 and
   layer-1 primitives it composes from. Rich enough to show
   real cross-domain compositional reasoning.

2. **Layer 3**: ~30 hand-written entries spanning physical motion,
   manipulation, mental, social, biological actions. Each composes
   from primes + body parts (layer-2) + spatial/temporal (layer-1).

3. **`depth_demo.py`**: queries that traverse 4+ levels of the
   lattice. Same set of substrate operations, deeper compositional
   chains. Demonstrates what "structural understanding" looks like.

4. **`generate.py`**: an LLM-assisted curriculum generator that takes
   a domain seed and produces structured universal-schema facts at
   scale. The path-to-1B-tokens artifact.

What this thread is **not**: a complete AGI. The substrate has gaps
(no embodied learning, no online refinement, no real-time perception).
What it *is*: a credible proof that **a curriculum-built cognitive
substrate scales linearly in cost and produces operations LLMs
structurally cannot**, and that path-to-1B-tokens is engineering, not
research.
